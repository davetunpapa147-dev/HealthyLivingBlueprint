What Is High Blood Pressure (Hypertension)? A Practical Guide

High blood pressure (hypertension) means the force of blood pushing against artery walls is higher than recommended. Hypertension increases risk of heart attack, stroke, kidney disease and other complications. This guide explains what numbers mean, common causes, tests, and evidence-based treatments and lifestyle strategies.

How blood pressure is measured

Systolic vs diastolic

Systolic is the pressure when the heart contracts; diastolic is the pressure when the heart relaxes. Current guideline thresholds vary by organization — see references.

Blood pressure categories

  • Normal: <120/80 mm Hg
  • Elevated: 120–129/<80 mm Hg
  • Stage 1 Hypertension: 130–139/80–89 mm Hg
  • Stage 2 Hypertension: ≥140/90 mm Hg

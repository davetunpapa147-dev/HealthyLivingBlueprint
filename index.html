Know Your Health — Evidence-Based Information

Our clinical team curates up-to-date, medically reviewed health information to help you understand, prevent, and manage common conditions.

⚠️ Heart Disease is the #1 cause of death in the United States — responsible for 1 in every 4 deaths. The good news: up to 80% of cardiovascular events are preventable with the right lifestyle changes and medical care.

⚠️ Warning Signs of a Heart Attack

  • Chest pressure, tightness, or squeezing pain
  • Pain radiating to arm, jaw, neck, or back
  • Shortness of breath at rest or with mild activity
  • Cold sweat, nausea, or lightheadedness
  • Unusual fatigue — especially in women

Call 911 immediately if you experience these symptoms.

✅ Know Your Numbers

Blood Pressure: <120/80 mmHg LDL Cholesterol: <100 mg/dL Resting Heart Rate: 60–100 bpm BMI: 18.5–24.9 Blood Sugar (fasting): <100 mg/dL

Schedule annual screenings with your primary care provider to monitor these key indicators.

🏃 Heart-Healthy Lifestyle Tips

  • 150 minutes of moderate aerobic exercise per week
  • Mediterranean or DASH diet — rich in vegetables, fish, and whole grains
  • Quit smoking — risk halves within 1 year of stopping
  • Limit alcohol to ≤2 drinks/day (men), ≤1 (women)
  • Manage stress with mindfulness, yoga, or therapy
  • Sleep 7–9 hours per night

📋 Recommended Heart Screenings

  • Ages 18+: Blood pressure every 1–2 years
  • Ages 35+ (men), 45+ (women): Cholesterol panel every 5 years
  • Ages 40–70 (overweight): Diabetes screening every 3 years
  • Ages 65+: Abdominal aortic aneurysm ultrasound (men who smoked)
  • High-risk patients: Coronary artery calcium (CAC) score

37.3 million Americans have diabetes — and 8.5 million of them are undiagnosed. Another 96 million adults have prediabetes. Early detection and management can prevent or delay serious complications.

⚠️ Symptoms of High Blood Sugar

  • Frequent urination, especially at night
  • Excessive thirst and dry mouth
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow-healing cuts or bruises
  • Tingling, numbness, or pain in hands/feet
  • Fatigue and irritability

📊 Blood Sugar Targets (ADA Guidelines)

Fasting (Non-diabetic): 70–99 mg/dL Prediabetes (Fasting): 100–125 mg/dL Diabetes (Fasting): ≥126 mg/dL HbA1c Target (Diabetic): <7%

The HbA1c test reflects your average blood sugar over the past 2–3 months and is the gold standard for diabetes monitoring.

💡 Managing Diabetes Daily

  • Test blood glucose as prescribed — fasting and post-meal
  • Follow a low-glycemic diet: whole grains, legumes, non-starchy vegetables
  • 30 minutes of walking daily can reduce HbA1c by 0.5–1%
  • Take medications and insulin as prescribed — never skip doses
  • Check feet daily for cuts, blisters, or numbness
  • Get annual eye, kidney, and nerve function exams

📋 Diabetes Screening Schedule

  • Age 35+, overweight: Screen every 3 years
  • Any age with risk factors: BMI ≥25, family history, gestational diabetes, hypertension
  • Pregnant women: GDM screen at 24–28 weeks
  • Diagnosed prediabetes: Retest annually

1 in 5 adults in the U.S. experiences a mental health condition each year. Despite this, nearly 60% never receive treatment. Mental health conditions are real medical conditions — and treatment works.

⚠️ Signs You May Need Support

  • Persistent sadness, emptiness, or hopelessness lasting 2+ weeks
  • Excessive worry or fear that interferes with daily life
  • Mood swings, irritability, or emotional outbursts
  • Withdrawal from friends, family, or activities you once enjoyed
  • Changes in sleep — insomnia or sleeping too much
  • Thoughts of self-harm or suicide — seek help immediately

🩺 Common Conditions We Treat

  • Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)
  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
  • Bipolar I and II Disorder
  • Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)
  • ADHD in adults and children
  • Eating disorders
  • Substance use disorders

💡 Evidence-Based Self-Care

  • Regular aerobic exercise reduces depression symptoms by 30–40%
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is as effective as medication for many conditions
  • Sleep hygiene: fixed wake time, no screens 1 hour before bed
  • Social connection — even brief daily interactions matter significantly
  • Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) reduces anxiety by up to 38%

1.9 million new cancer diagnoses are made in the U.S. each year. When caught early, many cancers have 5-year survival rates above 90%. Screening saves lives — don't wait for symptoms.

📋 Recommended Cancer Screenings

  • Breast cancer: Mammogram annually, starting at age 40 (or 30 with family history)
  • Colorectal cancer: Colonoscopy every 10 years starting at age 45
  • Cervical cancer: Pap smear every 3 years (age 21–65)
  • Lung cancer: Low-dose CT annually if 50+ with 20-pack-year smoking history
  • Prostate cancer: PSA discussion with doctor at age 50 (45 if high-risk)
  • Skin cancer: Annual full-body dermatology exam

⚠️ 7 Cancer Warning Signs (ACS)

  • C — Change in bowel or bladder habits
  • A — A sore that does not heal
  • U — Unusual bleeding or discharge
  • T — Thickening or lump in breast or elsewhere
  • I — Indigestion or difficulty swallowing
  • O — Obvious change in wart or mole
  • N — Nagging cough or hoarseness

🛡️ Evidence-Based Prevention

  • Quit smoking — causes 30% of all cancer deaths
  • Maintain healthy weight — obesity linked to 13 cancer types
  • Limit alcohol — every drink increases cancer risk slightly
  • Protect skin with SPF 30+ daily, avoid tanning beds
  • Get vaccinated: HPV vaccine prevents 6 cancer types
  • Eat a plant-rich diet — aim for 5 servings of fruits/vegetables daily

💊 Our Oncology Services

  • Low-dose CT lung cancer screening
  • 3D mammography (tomosynthesis)
  • Colonoscopy with same-day results discussion
  • Genetic cancer risk counseling (BRCA, Lynch syndrome)
  • Chemotherapy coordination and support
  • Cancer survivorship and follow-up care plans

Poor diet contributes to 678,000 deaths annually in the U.S., making it the leading behavioral risk factor for chronic disease. What you eat is one of the most powerful medicines available.

🥗 Evidence-Based Dietary Guidelines

  • Fill half your plate with fruits and vegetables at every meal
  • Choose whole grains (oats, quinoa, brown rice) over refined carbs
  • Lean proteins: fish, poultry, legumes, nuts, and tofu
  • Limit ultra-processed foods, added sugars, and trans fats
  • Reduce sodium to <2,300 mg/day (<1,500 if hypertensive)
  • Drink 8–10 cups of water daily; limit sugary drinks

📊 Key Nutritional Targets (Adults)

Fiber: 25g (women) / 38g (men) daily Protein: 0.8g per kg body weight Added Sugar: <25g/day (women), <36g (men) Saturated Fat: <10% of daily calories Vitamin D: 600–800 IU/day

Our registered dietitians create personalized meal plans based on your health goals, medical history, and food preferences.

🍽️ The Mediterranean Diet — Best Evidenced Pattern

  • Abundant: olive oil, vegetables, legumes, whole grains, fish
  • Moderate: poultry, eggs, dairy, red wine (optional)
  • Rare: red meat, processed foods, sweets
  • Associated with 25% lower cardiovascular risk (PREDIMED trial)
  • Reduces type 2 diabetes risk by up to 23%

⚠️ Signs of Nutritional Deficiency

  • Iron deficiency: Fatigue, pale skin, brittle nails, cold hands
  • Vitamin D deficiency: Bone pain, muscle weakness, depression
  • B12 deficiency: Numbness, memory issues, fatigue (especially vegans)
  • Magnesium deficiency: Muscle cramps, insomnia, anxiety

Women face unique health challenges throughout their lives — from reproductive health and hormonal changes to higher rates of autoimmune conditions. Proactive, personalized care makes all the difference.

📋 Women's Preventive Care Schedule

  • Annual: Well-woman exam, blood pressure, weight, depression screen
  • Every 1–3 years: Pap smear (age 21+), STI screening if sexually active
  • Every 1–2 years: Mammogram (age 40+)
  • Age 50+: Colonoscopy every 10 years, bone density scan (DXA)
  • Ongoing: Folic acid supplementation in reproductive-age women

🌸 Menopause — What to Expect

  • Average onset: age 51 (perimenopause begins 4–8 years earlier)
  • Hot flashes affect 75% of women; can last 7+ years
  • Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) is safe for most women under 60
  • Weight-bearing exercise protects bone density after menopause
  • Vaginal estrogen therapy relieves dryness with minimal systemic risk
  • Sleep disruption is common — CBT-I is the first-line treatment

⚠️ Symptoms That Need Attention

  • Abnormal uterine bleeding or postmenopausal bleeding
  • Pelvic pain or pressure lasting more than a few days
  • Breast lump, nipple discharge, or skin changes
  • Severe or sudden pelvic pain (possible ectopic pregnancy or ovarian torsion)
  • Urinary leakage, frequent infections, or painful urination

🤰 Prenatal Care Milestones

  • Week 8–10: First OB visit, confirmation ultrasound
  • Week 11–13: First trimester screening (NIPT, nuchal translucency)
  • Week 18–22: Anatomy scan ultrasound
  • Week 24–28: Gestational diabetes screening (GCT)
  • Week 36+: Weekly visits, GBS screening

Men die on average 5 years earlier than women and are far less likely to seek medical care. Regular check-ups catch silent conditions — and can add years to your life.

📋 Men's Preventive Screening Schedule

  • Annual: Blood pressure, BMI, lipid panel (if risk factors), depression screen
  • Age 35+: Fasting blood glucose every 3 years
  • Age 45+: Colorectal cancer screening; discuss PSA testing with your doctor
  • Age 65+: Abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA) ultrasound once if ever smoked
  • Age 50+: Lung cancer screening if 20-pack-year smoking history

🏃 Men's Top Preventable Health Risks

  • Heart disease: Leading cause of death in men — 1 in 4 deaths
  • Prostate cancer: Most common cancer in men; 1 in 8 diagnosed in lifetime
  • Testicular cancer: Most common in men aged 15–35; self-exams monthly
  • Colon cancer: 40% of cases are in men; colonoscopy at 45
  • Suicide: Men account for 78% of suicides; early mental health care matters

⚠️ Men's Health Warning Signs

  • Chest pain, shortness of breath, or pressure in jaw or arm
  • Difficulty urinating, weak stream, or blood in urine
  • Unexplained weight loss or fatigue
  • Lump in the testicle (even painless)
  • Sexual dysfunction or hormonal changes
  • Persistent sadness, anger, or emotional withdrawal

💪 Testosterone & Hormonal Health

  • Testosterone naturally declines ~1% per year after age 30
  • Low T symptoms: fatigue, low libido, brain fog, muscle loss, mood changes
  • Diagnosis requires two morning blood tests on separate days
  • TRT is an option for confirmed hypogonadism — benefits and risks should be discussed
  • Lifestyle first: weight loss, resistance training, sleep, and stress reduction all raise testosterone

Well-child visits and immunizations are among the most impactful health investments you can make. Children who receive regular preventive care have better health outcomes throughout their entire lives.

📋 Well-Child Visit Schedule (AAP)

  • Newborn: 3–5 days, 1 month, 2 months
  • Infancy: 4, 6, 9, 12 months
  • Toddler: 15, 18, 24, 30 months
  • Preschool: Age 3, 4, 5 years
  • School-age: Annual visit ages 6–21

💉 Childhood Immunization Milestones

  • Birth: Hepatitis B (dose 1)
  • 2 months: DTaP, Hib, IPV, PCV15, Rotavirus
  • 6 months: Influenza (annually thereafter)
  • 12–15 months: MMR, Varicella, Hep A
  • 4–6 years: DTaP, MMR, Varicella boosters
  • 11–12 years: HPV series, Tdap, MenACWY

⚠️ When to Seek Urgent Pediatric Care

  • Fever over 100.4°F in infants under 3 months
  • Difficulty breathing, fast breathing, or ribs showing
  • Stiff neck, severe headache, or light sensitivity with fever
  • Seizures — any first seizure
  • Significant head injury or loss of consciousness
  • Rash with fever (could indicate meningitis or measles)

🧒 Childhood Developmental Milestones

  • 2 months: Smiles at people, coos and makes sounds
  • 6 months: Sits with support, responds to name
  • 12 months: Says "mama/dada," pulls to stand
  • 24 months: 50+ words, follows 2-step instructions
  • 36 months: 3-word sentences, can dress with help
